Bar Graph:

-A bar graph uses rectangular bars to represent data.

-It is mainly used to compare values between different categories.

-The graph has both X-axis and Y-axis.

-The height or length of each bar indicates the value.

-It is easy to compare exact quantities using a bar graph.


Pie Chart:

-A pie chart is a circular graph divided into sectors (slices).

-It is used to show how different categories contribute to a whole.

-Each slice represents a percentage or proportion of the total.

-Pie charts do not use X-axis or Y-axis.

-They provide a quick visual understanding of data distribution.


Difference:

-Bar Graph: Best for comparing values.

-Pie Chart: Best for showing percentages and proportions.
